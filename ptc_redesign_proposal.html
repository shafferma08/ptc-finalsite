1. Executive Summary

This audit evaluates all three Pinellas Technical College websites — the PTC Main Site (myptc.edu), the Clearwater Campus (clearwater.myptc.edu), and the St. Petersburg Campus (stpete.myptc.edu) — against the official PTC brand guide, modern UX/UI best practices, COE accreditation requirements, and the capabilities of the Finalsite CMS platform.

While the current sites are functional, our audit uncovered systemic issues that undermine brand integrity, create maintenance overhead, and pose potential accreditation compliance risk:

3 Brand Violations
6+ Broken Links Found
200+ Pages Audited
COE Dedicated Page Planned
Critical Finding: Content Duplication The legacy architecture relies on copy-pasting identical Financial Aid, Admissions, Testing, and Distance Learning content across all three sites. This creates an immense maintenance burden: when one page is updated, the copies on the other two sites fall out of sync, leading to data rot and potential student confusion.

3. Navigation & UX Issues (Legacy)

All three legacy sites hide their entire navigation behind a hamburger menu — even on desktop. This is an anti-pattern for institutional websites where discoverability is critical for enrollment conversion.

3.1 Main Site (myptc.edu)

  • No visible desktop navigation bar — all content hidden behind a hamburger ☰ icon
  • "About Us" contains 12+ items nested 3 levels deep under a "Welcome to PTC!" sub-page
  • "Resources" mixes unrelated items (Student Resources, Career Center, Community Involvement, Unemployment Info)
  • Redundant links — "Campuses" appears in both the utility bar and the navigation
  • No dedicated "Admissions" or "Financial Aid" menu — the primary conversion pathways are buried

3.2 Campus Sites

  • Programs listed as a flat list of 25+ items with no categorization by career cluster
  • "Admissions > Admissions" redundant nesting in URLs
  • Inconsistent naming: "Student Services and Hours" (Clearwater) vs. "Student Services Hours" (St. Pete)
  • Placeholder URLs actively live on stpete.myptc.edu (/link-one, /link-two, /link-three)
  • St. Pete lunch menu URL is literally /menus/clw-menu-clone — a clone of Clearwater's menu page

3.3 Cross-Site Architecture

  • Campus links open in new browser tabs, creating a confusing multi-window experience
  • No unified navigation shell — main site and campus sites feel like entirely separate websites
  • "Return to MyPTC.edu" text on campus sites makes users feel they've left the PTC ecosystem

4. COE Accreditation & Compliance Strategy

PTC's footer legally states: "Each Pinellas Technical College campus is individually accredited by the Council on Occupational Education." This accreditation model means COE evaluates each campus website independently during review.

Key Finding (Confirmed with COE Contact) After consulting with our COE contact, we confirmed that the existing campus catalog already satisfies many of COE's publishing requirements for both website and print materials. Rather than treating this as a compliance gap, the opportunity is to create a dedicated COE Accreditation page on each campus site that brings this information together in one clear, accessible place — making it easy for reviewers and students to find what they need.

4.1 COE-Related Content Currently on the Website

Much of what COE looks for in website publishing materials is already present across the campus sites — often scattered across multiple pages. The table below maps existing content to relevant standards.

3Net Price Calculator (campus-specific tuition/fees)Federal (HEA)Published

4.2 Proposed: Dedicated COE Accreditation Page

The redesign will add a "Accreditation" page under School Information / About Us on each campus site. This page will consolidate and clarify COE-relevant content in one place, making it easy for accreditation reviewers to locate — rather than requiring them to check the catalog, footer, and multiple interior pages separately.

What the COE Page Will Include

  • COE accreditation statement with full contact info for COE
  • Links to program pages with descriptions, length, costs
  • Link to the campus catalog (which meets many additional standards)
  • Tuition & fee schedule / Net Price Calculator
  • Admissions and enrollment procedures
  • Student services summary and hours
  • Staff directory link

Why This Approach Works

  • Catalog already meets many COE publishing standards — no need to duplicate content
  • Centralizes scattered content for easy reviewer access
  • Low lift — mostly linking and organizing existing pages
  • Improves transparency for prospective students as a bonus
  • Scalable: each campus gets its own version with campus-specific links

8. Implementation Roadmap

The modernization follows a phased approach to manage risk, gather approval at each gate, and protect live site data during the transition.

Phase Objective Deliverables Status
Phase 1
Audit & Research		 Scrape and map the full page structure of all three sites. Verify brand compliance. Identify COE gaps. Site Audit Report, Complete Site Maps (200+ pages catalogued), Brand Comparison Matrix Completed
Phase 2
Strategy & Proposal		 Define the new 5-Pillar navigation model, campus vs. main site content ownership, and visual direction. This Redesign Proposal, Proposed Sitemaps, COE Compliance Matrix, UX Review Completed
Phase 3
HTML/CSS Mockups		 Build high-fidelity, responsive HTML templates for all page types before Finalsite implementation. Main Site Homepage ✓, Campus Homepages ✓, Interior Pages (Programs A-Z, Admissions, Financial Aid) In Progress
Phase 4
Finalsite Implementation		 Migrate approved HTML/CSS templates directly into the Finalsite Composer environment and launch. Live site deployment, QA testing, SEO validation, Accessibility audit Pending
