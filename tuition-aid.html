Tuition & Financial Aid

PTC is one of Florida's most affordable pathways to a career-ready credential. Explore our tuition rates, payment options, and financial aid resources to find the support that fits your situation.

Ready to Pay Your Tuition?

Current students can pay online through the Focus student portal. Questions? Contact the Business Office at your campus.

Tuition & Fee Rates

PTC offers some of the lowest tuition rates in the state. The tables below reflect standard rates for Florida residents. Non-resident rates and program-specific lab fees may vary.

Student Type Cost per Credit Hour Typical Program Length Estimated Total Tuition
Florida Resident $2.91/clock hour 900–1,800 hours ~$2,619–$5,238
Non-Resident $11.64/clock hour 900–1,800 hours ~$10,476–$20,952
Dual Enrollment (HS Students) No cost to student Varies by program $0
Rates are set by the Florida Legislature and subject to change. Lab fees, uniforms, tools, and certification exam costs are separate from tuition. Contact your campus Business Office for a complete cost estimate for your specific program.

Financial Aid Options

Most PTC students qualify for some form of financial assistance. Our financial aid counselors are here to help you navigate every option available.

FAFSA & Federal Aid

Filing the FAFSA is the first step toward most financial aid. PTC's Federal School Code is 013847. Many students qualify for Federal Pell Grants, which do not need to be repaid.

Complete the FAFSA at studentaid.gov

Scholarships

PTC and Pinellas County Schools offer a variety of scholarships for enrolled students, including industry-specific awards and general merit scholarships. Applications open each fall semester.

Browse Available Scholarships

State & Workforce Funding

Florida residents may qualify for additional state programs including Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) grants, which can cover tuition, books, and supplies for eligible students in high-demand programs.

Learn About State Programs

PTC's Business Office offers installment payment plans to help students manage costs semester by semester. Contact your campus Business Office to set up a payment arrangement before the term begins.

How Financial Aid Works at PTC

New to financial aid? Here is the basic process from start to funding.

Apply to PTC

Complete your admissions application and get accepted into a program. You must be enrolled to receive most types of financial aid.

File the FAFSA

Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid at studentaid.gov using PTC's school code (013847). This unlocks grants, scholarships, and work-study options.

Receive Your Award Letter

PTC's financial aid office will notify you of your aid package. Review it carefully and accept the awards you want to use.

Aid is Applied to Your Account

Accepted aid is applied directly to your tuition balance. Any remaining balance is your responsibility; any credit can be refunded to you.

Veterans & Military Benefits

PTC is proud to support our veteran and active-duty students. We are approved for multiple GI Bill programs and have dedicated staff to help you maximize your education benefits.

Contact Our Veterans Services Team
  • GI Bill Approved — PTC is approved for Chapter 30, 33 (Post-9/11), 35, and 1606 benefits
  • MyCAA Scholarships — Eligible military spouses can receive up to $4,000 toward education costs
  • Vocational Rehabilitation — Chapter 31 VR&E benefits can cover tuition, fees, and supplies for eligible veterans
  • Veterans Resource Coordinator — Dedicated on-campus support at Clearwater campus for benefits navigation and referrals
  • Tuition Assistance (TA) — Active-duty military members can use federal Tuition Assistance to cover program costs

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about costs or aid? Here are the ones we hear most often.

  • Grants (such as Federal Pell Grants and state grants) do not need to be repaid as long as you remain enrolled and maintain satisfactory academic progress. Scholarships are also generally not repaid. Student loans are a different story and do require repayment, so always prioritize grants and scholarships first. PTC's financial aid counselors can walk you through the difference.
  • PTC's Federal School Code is 013847. Enter this on your FAFSA to ensure your aid eligibility information is sent to our financial aid office. If you are applying for aid at both campuses, use the same school code — PTC is one institution with two locations.
  • Yes. In addition to per-hour tuition, most programs have lab fees, and you may need to purchase uniforms, tools, textbooks, or testing materials. Some certification exams also have separate fees. Ask your program advisor for a full cost breakdown before enrolling so there are no surprises.
  • Tuition refund eligibility depends on when you withdraw relative to the start of the term. Generally, a full refund is available during the first week of instruction. After that, refunds are prorated. If you received financial aid and withdraw, a portion of that aid may need to be returned. Contact the Business Office at your campus for the current refund schedule before making any decisions.
  • Federal financial aid (including Pell Grants) is available for most of PTC's full-length career certificate programs. Short-term, non-credit, or community education courses are generally not eligible for federal aid. Workforce funding programs such as WIOA may cover some short-term training. Ask a financial aid counselor about your specific program.
