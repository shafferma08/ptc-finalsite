About Pinellas Technical College

For over 60 years, PTC has been Pinellas County's trusted source for career and technical education, preparing students for high-demand jobs through hands-on, industry-certified training.

"To provide a positive learning environment that prepares a diverse student population for successful employment through relevant career and technical education."

Our Mission

Our Story

Pinellas Technical College opened its doors in 1961 to meet the growing demand for skilled workers in the Tampa Bay region. What started as a single vocational campus has grown into a two-campus institution serving thousands of students each year.

Today, PTC offers more than 40 career and technical programs across two campuses in Clearwater and St. Petersburg. Our programs span health sciences, skilled trades, information technology, culinary arts, cosmetology, transportation, and more. Every program is designed around real-world skills that employers need right now.

As part of Pinellas County Schools, PTC combines the resources of a public school system with the focused, career-driven mission of a technical college. Students benefit from affordable tuition, financial aid, and direct connections to local employers.

Where We Come From

Our History

1961

Pinellas Technical Education Center opens its doors, offering vocational training to the Clearwater community.

1970s

The St. Petersburg campus is established, expanding access to career and technical education across the county.

1990s

Both campuses expand program offerings to include health sciences, information technology, and advanced manufacturing.

2000s

PTC earns initial accreditation from the Council on Occupational Education (COE), affirming the quality of its programs and student outcomes.

2018

Pinellas Technical Education Centers are rebranded as Pinellas Technical College, reflecting the institution's growth and the college-level rigor of its programs.

Today

PTC serves thousands of students annually across 40+ programs, with strong job placement rates and deep ties to Tampa Bay employers.

Quality You Can Trust

Accreditation

PTC's accreditation status ensures that our programs meet rigorous quality standards and that your credentials are recognized by employers and institutions.

Council on Occupational Education

Both the Clearwater and St. Petersburg campuses are individually accredited by COE, the nationally recognized accrediting body for career and technical education institutions.

Cognia (AdvancED)

As part of Pinellas County Schools, PTC benefits from district-wide accreditation by Cognia, ensuring continuous improvement in educational quality.

Pinellas County Schools

PTC operates under the governance of the Pinellas County School Board, one of the largest school districts in Florida, providing institutional stability and resources.

Two Campuses, One Mission

PTC's Clearwater and St. Petersburg campuses each offer a unique mix of programs, but both share the same commitment to preparing students for rewarding careers.

The Clearwater Campus at 6100 154th Ave N is home to health sciences, automotive technology, welding, HVAC, and more. The St. Petersburg Campus at 901 34th St S features culinary arts, cosmetology, IT networking, electrical, and commercial vehicle programs.

No matter which campus you choose, you will have access to modern labs, experienced instructors, and direct connections to Tampa Bay employers.
