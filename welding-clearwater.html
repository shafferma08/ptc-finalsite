About This Program

Program J400400 consists of a planned sequence of courses totaling 1,050 clock hours.

The Welder course builds on the skills and knowledge students learned in the Welder Assistant and Welder SMAW courses. Students explore career opportunities and requirements of a professional welder in the fabrication, repair, and construction industries.

Course Sequence

Click each phase to see what you’ll learn. Courses must be completed in order.

Welder Assistant 2 (PMT0071) 150 Hours

The Welder Assistant 2 course is designed to build on the skills and knowledge students learned in Welder Assistant 1 for entry into the welding industry. Students study drawings and welding symbols, intermediate oxyfuel gas cutting practices, plasma arc cutting principles, and basic shielded metal arc welding (SMAW).

Welder, SMAW 1 (PMT0072) 150 Hours

Prepares students for entry into the industry as a basic Shielded Metal Arc Welder. Students study basic shielded metal arc welding (SMAW), Carbon Arc Gouging (GAC) principles, and visual examination skills.

Welder, SMAW 2 (PMT0073) 150 Hours

Builds on the skills learned in Welder SMAW 1. Students study employability and welding careers, and intermediate shielded metal arc welding (SMAW).

Welder (PMT0074) 450 Hours

The final phase builds on previous courses. Students study basic and intermediate Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW), basic and intermediate Flux-Core Arc Welding (FCAW), basic and intermediate Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW), and a basic understanding of pipe welding.

Industry Credentials

American Welding Society (AWS)

After completing all competencies, students are encouraged and eligible to take the American Welding Society (AWS) certification test. There is an application fee from AWS. The Clearwater campus of Pinellas Technical College is an AWS accredited testing facility.

Florida Ready to Work

The goal of Pinellas Technical College’s career and technical programs is for each student to achieve an industry-recognized credential or professional license. It is an expectation that students will earn the FL Ready to Work Soft Skills credential before graduating. The Digital Skills credential is also highly recommended as all careers use technology. The Employability Skills credential reviews basic reading and math skills used on the job.

These credentials align with our BIG SIX initiative — professionalism, pride, focus, success, ownership, and excellence. myfloridareadytowork.com →
