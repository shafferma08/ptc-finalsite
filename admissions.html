Admissions

Getting started at PTC is straightforward. Whether you are a first-time student, transferring from another school, or returning after a break, we will guide you through every step.

Get Started

How to Apply

Applying to PTC is free and takes just a few steps. Most students can complete the process in a single visit.

1

Choose Your Program

Browse our 40+ career programs to find the one that matches your goals. Check campus availability, schedule options, and program length.

2

Submit Your Application

Complete the online application. You will need proof of residency, a valid ID, and your high school diploma or equivalent. There is no application fee.

3

Meet with a Counselor

If needed, meet with a school counselor (admissions contact) to discuss program interests and requirements. You can connect with a counselor before or after applying.

Start Your Application

What to Expect

Enrollment Steps

After your application is accepted, here is what comes next.

Complete Required Testing

Some programs require placement testing (CASAS or TEAS). Your counselor will let you know what testing is needed for your program.

Arrange Financial Aid

Complete your FAFSA and meet with the financial aid office to explore grants, scholarships, veterans benefits, and payment plans.

Register for Classes

Work with your counselor to select your start date and register for your program. Attend new student orientation to get set up for day one.

Your Path to PTC

Admission Pathways

Transfer Students

Transferring from another technical school or college? You may be able to receive credit for prior coursework. Bring your official transcripts to your admissions meeting.

Readmission

Took a break and want to come back? If you were previously enrolled at PTC, you can apply for readmission. Contact your campus admissions office to get started.

Dual Enrollment

High school students can get a head start on their career by enrolling in PTC programs while still in high school. Talk to your high school counselor to see if you qualify.

Veterans

PTC proudly serves veterans and active-duty military. We accept VA education benefits and have dedicated veterans resources on both campuses.

Veterans benefits info

Placement Testing

Testing & Assessment

Some programs require a placement test before enrollment. Your admissions counselor will confirm which test, if any, applies to your program.

CASAS

Comprehensive Adult Student Assessment System. Required for most career and technical programs. Measures reading, math, and listening skills.

TEAS

Test of Essential Academic Skills. Required for health science programs including Practical Nursing, Patient Care Technician, and Surgical Technology.

ABE / GED / ESOL

If you need to complete your GED or improve your English language skills, PTC offers Adult Basic Education and ESOL programs at both campuses.

Visit Our Campuses

See our labs, meet instructors, and learn about programs firsthand. Campus tours and shadowing days are available at both locations.

Schedule a Tour

Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an application fee?
No. Applying to PTC is completely free. There are no application or enrollment fees.
Do I need a high school diploma or GED?
Most career and technical programs require a high school diploma or GED equivalent. However, PTC also offers Adult Basic Education (ABE) and GED preparation programs if you need to complete that requirement first.
When can I start?
Many PTC programs have multiple start dates throughout the year. Your admissions counselor can provide specific start dates for the program you are interested in. Some programs have rolling enrollment.
Is financial aid available?
Yes. PTC participates in federal and state financial aid programs, including Pell Grants, Florida Student Assistance Grants, scholarships, and veterans benefits. Complete your FAFSA to find out what you qualify for.
Can I attend part-time or in the evening?
Some programs offer evening and part-time scheduling options. Availability varies by program and campus. Check the program page or ask your counselor for details.

Accommodations

Accommodations are available during the instructional program for students with documented physical or mental impairments. Please see a school counselor for further information.
