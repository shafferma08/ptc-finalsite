# PTC Homepage UX & Flow Review

As requested, this is a comprehensive user experience (UX) review of the newly designed Pinellas Technical College (PTC) homepage. This review analyzes the navigation, page flow, and content hierarchy from the perspective of our core stakeholders.

## 1. Executive Summary
The transition to a "credibility-first" flow (Why PTC → Programs → Campuses) has vastly improved the narrative of the site. It establishes trust before asking users to commit to exploring a program. Overall, the layout is highly converting, clean, and accessible. However, while the site heavily serves prospective students, there are minor opportunities to better surface paths for current students and community employers.

---

## 2. Stakeholder Journey Mapping

### 👤 Prospective Students
**Goal:** Discover programs, compare campuses, check costs, and apply.
*   **The Flow:** Excellent. They are immediately presented with "Your Career Starts Here" and a hero "Explore/Apply" CTA. 
*   **Quick Links:** Perfectly aligned with their needs (Apply, Programs, Tuition, Visit, Events).
*   **Navigation:** "Admissions" and the newly added "Tuition & Aid" dropdowns remove the friction of hunting for financial information.
*   **Verdict:** **A+**. The page operates as a high-conversion landing page for enrollment.

### 🎓 Current Students
**Goal:** Access portals, view schedules, pay tuition, or seek student services.
*   **The Flow:** Their primary gateway is the Utility Bar at the very top right ("Student Portal"). This is a standard higher-education pattern.
*   **Navigation:** They will likely skip the hero and quick links and rely on the Utility Bar or Footer. The Footer includes a "Student Resources" column which is great.
*   **Verdict:** **B+**. The flow is functional, but the main visual real estate is dedicated to enrollment. 
*   *Recommendation:* Consider adding "Student Portal" or "Current Students" to the Quick Links icon grid so they don't have to rely solely on the tiny utility link.

### 🤝 Community & Employers
**Goal:** Hire graduates, partner with the school, or volunteer.
*   **The Flow:** The dedicated "Community" dropdown in the main navigation is a massive win. It gives employers a clear path ("Employer Partnerships", "Post a Job").
*   **Content:** The News & Events section highlights PTC's community involvement (e.g., Safe Harbor volunteering) which builds institutional prestige.
*   **Verdict:** **A-**. Very clear navigation, but they lack a visual CTA in the middle of the page.

---

## 3. Section-by-Section Breakdown

### Utility Bar & Main Header
*   **Strengths:** Clear separation between "Utility" (Portals, District Home, Phones) and "Core Nav" (Programs, Admissions). Search bar overlay is accessible and modern.
*   **Critique:** "Student Portal" is in the utility bar, but is it just one portal? Often students need Canvas, Focus, and Webmail. 

### Hero Section & Quick Links
*   **Strengths:** The 100vh height ensures the value proposition is seen without scrolling. The Ken Burns animated slider feels premium. The quick links act as an excellent "shortcut" menu for power users.
*   **Critique:** The quick links are entirely prospect-focused. 

### Core Content (Why PTC, Programs, Campuses)
*   **Strengths:** The chronological flow is brilliant: 
    1. **Why us?** (Accreditation, flexible schedule).
    2. **What do we teach?** (Program Categories).
    3. **Where do we teach it?** (Campuses).
*   **Critique:** Under the "Campuses" section, we have buttons for "Visit Clearwater Campus". Ensure these buttons clearly communicate whether they link to the campus subdomain homepage or a physical tour booking page.

### News, Events, and CTA Band
*   **Strengths:** Pulling real dynamic content keeps the page feeling "alive." The dark CTA band grounds the end of the page and catches users before they hit the footer.

### Footer
*   **Strengths:** Very structured. The three columns (Quick Links, Campuses, Resources) make sense. The Accreditation badges (COE, Cognia, PCS) are prominently displayed, satisfying compliance requirements and boosting trust.
*   **Critique:** Under "Resources", there is a link for "Employment". It's ambiguous if this means "Jobs at PTC for Staff" or "Employment for Students". 

---

## 4. Actionable Expert Recommendations

To achieve true perfection before we implement, I recommend the following minor tweaks to our mockup:

1.  **Enhance the Quick Links:** Swap one of the prospect-heavy quick links (like "Events") for a **"Current Students"** or **"Student Portal"** quick link. This acknowledges the massive current student base that visits the homepage daily.
2.  **Clarify Footer Links:** Rename "Employment" in the footer to **"Careers at PTC"** or **"Work for Us"** to distinguish it from the "Post a Job" link for employers in the Community nav.
3.  **Add a "Hire Our Grads" Hook:** If space permits in the future, adding a small callout box near the News section saying *"Are you an employer? Hire a PTC grad."* would drastically increase employer engagement.
4.  **Tuition & Aid Dropdown:** Ensure we include a link to *"Pay Tuition"* or *"Business Office"* in that dropdown, as current students look there for payment portals.

These adjustments are minor text/link changes that will significantly elevate the overall usability for non-prospective stakeholders without diluting our primary goal of enrollment.
