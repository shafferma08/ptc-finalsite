# Parent/Guardian Review - 2026-04-22

**Reviewer:** Sandra, 45, mother of a 17-year-old considering automotive technology at PTC  
**Review Date:** April 22, 2026  
**Motivation:** Determine if PTC is a safe, reputable investment vs. 4-year college or community college

---

## Trust & Credibility

**POSITIVE - The site establishes legitimacy quickly:**
- Professional appearance with clean design and PTC branding throughout
- **Accreditation is VISIBLE** - I can see COE (Council on Occupational Education) and Cognia logos prominently in the footer on every page
- About page clearly states 60+ years of operation (since 1961), which signals stability and track record
- Connection to Pinellas County Schools is prominently featured - adds institutional backing and accountability
- Mission statement is clear and mission-focused ("prepare students for successful employment")

**CONCERN - Critical gaps undermine credibility:**
- **Leadership section on About page is completely hollow** (C3 - Open issue). Three "leader cards" show generic user icons with titles like "Campus Director" but NO NAMES. As a parent, I need to know who's actually running this place. This feels sketchy - like they're hiding something or didn't bother to fill it in.
- **All accreditation links appear non-functional** (C4 - Open issue). I clicked "About PTC" > "Accreditation" and found detailed accreditation cards, but the footer shows links to "Privacy Policy," "Accessibility," and "Consumer Information" that don't work. If I can't verify compliance documents, I'm uneasy.
- **No job outcomes or salary data anywhere** (H4 - Open issue). The site claims "graduates don't just earn certificates — they launch careers" but provides zero evidence. Where's the job placement rate? Salary range? Employer feedback? I'm comparing this to a 4-year university that publishes graduation rates and alumni outcomes. This is a dealbreaker for me.

**Verdict on Trust:** Site looks professional but feels incomplete. The hollow leadership section is a red flag.

## Cost & Value

**POSITIVE - Some financial aid info is present:**
- "Tuition & Aid" is accessible in main navigation and has a dedicated page
- Footer mentions "Affordable Tuition" in why-ptc section
- Admissions page FAQ confirms financial aid is available (FAFSA, Pell Grants, scholarships, veterans benefits)
- Quick links prominently feature "Tuition & Aid" on homepage

**CRITICAL CONCERNS - The value proposition is unclear:**
- **Tuition rates page exists but I can't see actual costs in the HTML preview** (H6 - Open issue). The page mentions "Tuition & Fee Rates" but doesn't display a table with actual dollar amounts. How much does automotive technology cost? Per semester? Total for the program? I need specifics to compare against community college or a university program.
- **No cost-of-attendance breakdown.** Is housing included? Books? Tools? For trade programs, tools can be expensive.
- **No Net Price Calculator** mentioned in the tuition-aid page structure. Many colleges offer this; PTC doesn't appear to.
- **Financial aid section incomplete.** The page acknowledges "Federal & State Funding" and "Scholarships" but doesn't explain which scholarships PTC offers or how much they cover.
- **No comparison to 4-year college ROI.** PTC is positioning itself as faster/cheaper than a 4-year degree, but I don't see concrete numbers showing: time to completion (6 months? 2 years?), total cost, typical entry-level salary after graduation, vs. cost of a bachelor's degree elsewhere.

**Verdict on Cost & Value:** I can see financial aid exists, but without actual tuition rates and job outcomes, I can't calculate whether this is a good investment for my son.

## Outcomes

**CRITICAL ISSUE - This is the biggest dealbreaker (H4, C1):**

The entire site is built on promises of "careers" and "job-ready" graduates, but provides **zero evidence of actual outcomes:**

- **No job placement rates.** What % of graduates are employed within 6 months? 1 year?
- **No salary data.** What does an automotive technology graduate earn in the Tampa Bay market? Entry level? After 5 years?
- **No employer testimonials or partnerships** (visible on homepage). The site mentions "50+ Industry Partners" but never names them or shows what they think of PTC graduates.
- **No student testimonials or success stories.** Where are the "Meet Our Graduates" profiles? Video testimonials?
- **No program-specific outcomes.** I can find Welding program pages that show gorgeous photos and instructor names, but nothing about: class size, hands-on hours, certification exam pass rates, job placement in the trade.
- **Homepage claims "40+ Career Programs" but provides no program detail pages except Welding** (H1 - Open issue). How do I know if Automotive Technology is being offered next semester? What are the prerequisites? How long is the program?

**What I would expect to see:**
- "85% of graduates are employed or continuing education within 6 months"
- "Average entry-level salary: $XX,XXX"
- "Our graduates work at [Big Employer Names]"
- Individual program pages showing: length, cost, certification, job titles, typical salary range

**Verdict on Outcomes:** The site makes big promises but provides no proof. This feels like a "trust us" pitch, and I don't trust institutions that won't show their work. If PTC's job placement is strong, why hide it?

## Summary

**The PTC website is professionally designed and covers the right topics, but it's incomplete in critical ways that undermine parent trust and enrollment.**

The site successfully establishes:
- Institutional credibility (60+ years, accredited by COE, part of PCSB)
- Clear mission (prepare students for high-demand careers)
- Multiple programs and two campuses
- Availability of financial aid

But it fails to deliver:
- **Proof of outcomes** (job placement, salary, success stories) — THE most important factor for parents
- **Actual costs** (tuition rates, program-specific pricing)
- **Campus logistics** (hours, parking, safety, facilities)
- **Program details** (curriculum, certification, job titles, prerequisites)
- **Leadership transparency** (names and faces of decision-makers)
- **Working application system** (can't actually apply online)

**As Sandra, my assessment:**
*"The site looks legitimate and professional, but I feel like I'm being sold a story instead of being shown facts. Before I invest in PTC for my son's career, I need to see: (1) job placement rates and salary data, (2) actual tuition costs, (3) proof that the campus is a safe place to study, and (4) a working way to actually apply. Until then, I'm not convinced this is better than the community college where I can see all that information online."*

**Net result:** Credible institution, but incomplete website that loses enrollment to competitors with more transparency.
