# Prospective Student Review (LIVE SITE) - 2026-04-15

**Reviewer Profile:** Jaylen, 19-year-old high school graduate, first-generation college student, interested in skilled trades, works part-time at a restaurant.

---

## First Impressions (Homepage)

**Landing on www.myptc.edu:**
- Visual impact: Strong hero image with graduation/students in caps and gown with tagline "Your Moment is Coming"
- Clear messaging: "Opportunity starts here" is immediate and welcoming
- Messaging clarity: Within 5 seconds, I can tell this is a technical/career college offering skilled trades
- Credibility cue: "Accredited. Affordable. Career-Ready" appears in the hero carousel
- Key stats visible: "60+ Programs. 40+ Career Paths" on main carousel shows volume of options
- Immediate action items: Right-side floating CTA buttons (INQUIRE, APPLY, STP Dual Enrollment, CLW Dual Enrollment) are prominent and sticky

**Verdict:** Strong first impression. The site clearly communicates this is a real, accredited institution focused on career training. However, the hero carousel requires active clicking to see all messages (some students might miss them).

---

## Finding a Program

**Ease of browsing:**
- Programs are accessible from both campus-specific sites (clearwater.myptc.edu and stpete.myptc.edu)
- No need to know exact program name to start browsing
- Clearwater campus: ~25+ full-time programs listed including trade skills (Diesel Maintenance Technician, Electrician, HVAC, Carpentry, Welding) and business/healthcare roles (Accounting, Medical Administrative Specialist, Network Support)
- St. Petersburg campus: Different program mix, appears nursing/healthcare-focused with programs like Practical Nursing, Medical Administrative Specialist
- Programs are organized by category (Full-Time, Evening/Short Courses, Apprenticeships, Dual Enrollment, etc.)

**Navigation quality:**
- Left sidebar menu on programs page makes browsing logical
- Can expand sections to see full program lists
- Brochure available for Clearwater Campus
- Program Shadowing Schedule link shows commitment to student exploration

**Issue identified:** The two campuses offer distinctly different programs, but the website doesn't make this immediately obvious. A prospective student might not realize they need to check both campuses to see all options. Would benefit from a comparison tool or note like "See different programs at each campus."

---

## Understanding Costs

**Tuition information location:** Found under Clearwater campus > Admissions > Financial Aid > Fees and Expenses

**Cost clarity: MODERATE**

Tuition rates found:
- Career Technical Certificate: $2.92/hour (residents), $11.71/hour (nonresidents/foreign students)
- Adult General Education (ESOL/GED): $45/term for residents (max $90/year), $120/term for nonresidents (max $360/year)
- Fee-Based and Self-Sustaining programs: Costs determined by facility and course

**Additional fees charged:**
- Application/processing fee
- ID card
- Parking permit
- Books, tools, supplies
- Laboratory fees
- Equipment and facility usage fees

**Financial aid available:** Yes, mentioned as available to those who qualify

**Major issue:** NO specific dollar amounts shown for most programs. The hourly rate for certificates is hard to convert to a total program cost without knowing program length. A prospective student like me would struggle to answer "How much will this actually cost?" Most important information is missing. Would need to contact admissions or use Net Price Calculator (mentioned in sidebar but not prominently).

**What's missing:**
- Total program cost examples (e.g., "Electrician Program: 1,200 hours = $3,504 for residents")
- Clear breakdown of average total costs by program type
- Financing options, payment plans, or tuition assistance programs

## Top 3 Issues (Ranked by Impact on My Decision to Enroll)

### 1. **CRITICAL: No Specific Tuition Amounts (Highest Impact)**
   - **Why it matters:** As a first-generation student, I need concrete numbers to make a decision and plan financially. Seeing "$2.92/hour" is meaningless without knowing program length or total cost.
   - **Current state:** Site lists hourly rates and general categories but not actual program costs
   - **Fix needed:** Provide real examples like "Electrician Program costs $3,500 for residents (1,200 hours @ $2.92/hour)" and show total cost ranges per program in a table
   - **Impact:** Without this, I would likely call or email admissions, delaying my decision

### 2. **SIGNIFICANT: Program Differences Between Campuses Not Obvious (Medium-High Impact)**
   - **Why it matters:** I found completely different program selections at Clearwater vs. St. Pete, but the website doesn't make this clear upfront
   - **Current state:** Each campus lists its own programs, but no comparison or guidance on which campus to choose for my interests
   - **Fix needed:** Either (a) unified program browser showing which programs are at which campus, or (b) "Program Guide: Choose Your Campus" that maps interests to campuses
   - **Impact:** Risk that I pick the "wrong" campus and don't see programs I'd actually want

### 3. **MODERATE: Accreditation & Job Outcomes Not Prominent (Medium Impact)**
   - **Why it matters:** As a first-generation student, I need proof this degree is worth my money and will lead to jobs. The site says "Accredited" in hero but accreditation details are buried
   - **Current state:** Accreditation mentioned on main page but no details on what it means or link to verification; no job placement rates or graduate testimonials visible on homepage
   - **Fix needed:** Add "60+ programs, 98% job placement rate" or similar stat to homepage; link accreditation statement to Council on Occupational Education verification
   - **Impact:** I might assume it's less credible than traditional colleges; no confidence in ROI
