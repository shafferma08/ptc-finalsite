# Faculty Review (LIVE SITE) - 2026-04-15

**Reviewer:** Robert, CNC Machining Instructor, PTC St. Petersburg (8 years)  
**Review Scope:** Three-site survey (myptc.edu, stpete.myptc.edu, clearwater.myptc.edu)  
**Focus:** Directing students to forms, schedules, campus info, and program visibility

---

## Program Representation

**CNC Machining Visibility Issue (CRITICAL)**
- CNC Machining program **does not appear on St. Petersburg campus program listings** (checked stpete.myptc.edu/programs/st-petersburg-program-offerings)
- Program **is listed on Clearwater campus site** (found on clearwater.myptc.edu/programs/clearwater-full-time-programs)
- Student impact: New students searching for "where is the CNC program?" will not find it on the St. Petersburg site, creating confusion about campus vs. program location
- Root cause likely: Program-to-campus mapping is incorrect or St. Petersburg programs page is incomplete

**Main Site Strengths**
- Homepage has clear "60+ Programs. 40+ Career Paths." messaging
- Campus links prominently displayed on homepage and in main navigation
- Calendar access is easy (main nav > Pinellas Technical College Calendar)

## Directing Students

**Apply Process - Clear**
- "Apply" button/link consistently available in header utility nav and main nav
- Points to apply.myptc.edu (verified via JavaScript)
- Single, obvious entry point for new students

**Student Links Page - Exists But Minimal Content**
- Located at myptc.edu/student-links (easy to find from main nav)
- Content sparse: only shows three key links:
  - Canvas Login (link works)
  - SIS Portal for PTC Students (pinellas.focusschoolsoftware.com/focus/)
  - Barnes & Noble | PTC's Virtual Bookstore (bncvirtual.com/ptc)
- Missing from Student Links page (based on accessibility tree):
  - Email addresses for tech support or student services
  - How to contact the campus by department
  - FAQs about registration, drop/add, financial aid
  - Links to campus directories or staff rosters
- **Faculty gap:** When I direct a student to "Student Links," they get basic portal access but not the help they might need (e.g., "Who do I talk to about withdrawing?" "How do I order a parking pass?")

**Program Pages - Not Unified**
- Clearwater and St. Petersburg have separate program listing pages but navigation differs
- Main site lacks a direct "All Programs" page linking to both campuses
- Students must choose a campus first before seeing programs

## Top 3 Issues (Ranked by Frequency of Student Confusion)

### 1. **Program Location Discrepancy** (HIGH IMPACT)
**Issue:** CNC Machining is not listed on stpete.myptc.edu/programs but is associated with St. Petersburg campus (Robert is the instructor there). Student searching "where is CNC" on St. Pete site will NOT find it; creates immediate confusion about which campus offers what.

**Frequency:** Any new student researching the program before applying.

**Fix needed:** Ensure all campus program pages list programs accurately. If a program is offered at only one campus, that must be consistent across the site. Consider adding a master program directory on main site linking to both campuses.

---

### 2. **Minimal Student Links / Sparse Help Resources** (MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT)
**Issue:** Student Links page shows only three portals (Canvas, SIS, Bookstore). Students asking "where do I go for financial aid questions?" "how do I contact my advisor?" "what are drop/add deadlines?" will not find answers on the site. When faculty direct students there, students hit a dead end.

**Frequency:** Every student during their first semester, especially first-time college students.

**Fix needed:** Expand Student Links with email contacts by department, FAQ links, campus-specific resources (parking, dining, IT help desk), and links to student services office pages. Include "Frequently Asked Questions" and "Contact a Student Advisor" sections.

---

### 3. **Faculty Resources Buried/Missing** (MEDIUM IMPACT)
**Issue:** No dedicated faculty section on main site. Finding PD course requests, AI Committee info, Canvas admin support, or peer contact directories requires clicking through "Resources" (if that has faculty content) or knowing exactly where to look. Faculty new to the college or new to these roles have no clear landing page.

**Frequency:** Regular faculty need (especially new hires, PLN course coordinators, AI Committee members).

**Fix needed:** Add "Faculty & Staff" top-level nav item or expand Resources section with faculty-specific links (PLN, Canvas support, professional development, staff directory, faculty news, curriculum resources).

---
