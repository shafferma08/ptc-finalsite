# Parent/Guardian Review (LIVE SITE) - 2026-04-15

## Trust & Credibility

### Accreditation Status
PTC Clearwater campus is individually accredited by the Council on Occupational Education and Cognia (9115 Westside Parkway, Alpharetta, GA 30009, 888-413-3669). This is legitimate, verifiable accreditation for career/technical education programs. Parents should know this is NOT regional university accreditation—it's specialized accreditation for occupational education, which is appropriate and standard for technical colleges.

### Public Recognition
- Listed on USA Today's 2025 list of America's Top 250 Vocational Schools (verified on homepage)
- This is a credible third-party recognition that adds weight to claims about program quality
- Good sign that employers and institutions view PTC as a reputable technical training provider

### Institutional Structure
- Part of Pinellas County Schools (PCSB)—not a private for-profit entity
- Government-run technical college under the district's Career and Technical Education (CTAE) program
- This means lower risk of predatory practices compared to private trade schools

## Cost & Value

### Tuition Rates (Current)
- **Career Technical Certificate programs: $2.92/hour** for Pinellas County residents, **$11.71/hour** for non-residents
- Programs vary in length; estimate roughly 1000-2000+ hours depending on the program
- **Rough cost estimate for residents:** $2,920–$5,840 for tuition alone
- **Non-resident estimate:** $11,710–$23,420 for tuition alone

### Additional Fees & Expenses
Parents MUST budget for:
- Application/processing fee
- Student ID card
- Parking permit
- Books, tools, and supplies (can be substantial for automotive work)
- Laboratory fees
- Equipment and facility usage fees
- Graduation fees
- Uniforms (as required by program)
- Certification/licensure exam assessments (required for many programs)

**Bottom line:** Total out-of-pocket cost will likely be $4,000–$7,000+ for residents over the program duration. This is significantly cheaper than a community college associate degree (typically $10,000–$15,000+) or a 4-year university.

### Financial Aid Available
- FAFSA/FSA eligibility help
- Net Price Calculator available on site (parents can estimate costs)
- Federal and State funding options
- Scholarships (PCSB scholarships available)
- Veterans Benefits (if applicable)
- Florida Prepaid tuition cards accepted (tuition fees covered; fees/supplies still due)

### Payment Structure
- **Critical note:** Tuition and fees are due **by the first day of class each term**
- Students MUST come to the bookstore to pay fees each term—automatic payment does NOT happen
- Multiple start dates per year (August, October, January, March/April, June)
- This flexibility is good for working parents or students with other commitments

## Top 3 Issues (Ranked by Impact on Trust and Enrollment Decision)

### 1. MISSING PROGRAM-SPECIFIC OUTCOMES DATA (High Impact)
**The Problem:** The website does not publish job placement rates, graduate salaries, or employer hiring data for ANY program, including Master Automotive Service Technology. This is a major gap for a parent paying thousands of dollars.

**Why it matters:** Community colleges and universities publish outcomes; PTC should too. Without this data, parents cannot assess whether the program actually leads to employment or competitive wages.

**What to do:** Contact the admissions office directly and ask:
- What percentage of Master Automotive graduates are employed within 6 months?
- What is the average starting salary in the field?
- Which specific employers regularly hire from the program?
- How does the automotive program rank among PTC's offerings for employment outcomes?

**Recommendation:** Request this data in writing and compare to community college programs before enrolling.

---

### 2. COST OPACITY FOR SPECIFIC PROGRAMS (Medium-High Impact)
**The Problem:** The website lists tuition ($2.92/hour for residents) but does NOT break down total program costs by program or provide estimated totals. Tool/supply costs for automotive can be substantial, and students don't know what they'll actually pay.

**Why it matters:** A parent commits to a significant investment without knowing the total cost. Different programs (nursing, welding, automotive) likely have very different fee structures.

**What to do:** Before shadowing:
- Ask for a written cost estimate for Master Automotive Service Technology
- Confirm whether tools/equipment are school-provided or student-purchased
- Ask if all students pay the same fees or if automotive has higher lab/facility fees
- Get a breakdown of supplies/books costs

**Recommendation:** Get written cost confirmation from the admissions office before deciding.

---

### 3. INSUFFICIENT DETAIL ON AUTOMOTIVE PROGRAM SPECIFICS (Medium Impact)
**The Problem:** The program exists (Master Automotive Service Technology 1 & 2 were found), but program length, curriculum, certification pathways, and prerequisite skills are not clearly described on the live site.

**Why it matters:** Parents need to know:
- How long is the program? (months/hours)
- What certifications does the student earn? (ASE certification? State licensing?)
- Is hands-on training with real vehicles or simulators?
- What's the classroom vs. shop ratio?
- Does the program guarantee any specific job outcomes?

**What to do:** Schedule a program shadowing visit and ask the instructor:
- What do graduates do after completing the program?
- How many students complete vs. drop out?
- What tools/certifications do students earn?
- Are there pathways to further education (community college, bachelor's)?

**Recommendation:** This is critical—don't enroll without understanding the program's concrete outcomes.

## Summary for Sandra (Parent Decision Framework)

**PTC is a SAFE, REPUTABLE option** compared to predatory private trade schools. It's government-run, accredited, and recognized by USA Today. Cost is reasonable ($4k–$7k vs. $15k+ for community college).

**HOWEVER, before enrolling your 17-year-old in Master Automotive, you MUST:**

1. Get written job placement data and salary info (non-negotiable)
2. Confirm total program costs in writing
3. Tour the campus, shadow the automotive program, and talk to current students/graduates
4. Ask hard questions about what happens after graduation

**Red flags to watch for:**
- If admissions can't or won't provide job placement data, that's a concern
- If cost estimates keep changing, ask for it in writing
- If the campus feels unsafe or disorganized, trust your instinct

**Green flags:**
- Accreditation is legitimate
- USA Today recognition is real
- Cost is transparent and reasonable
- Admissions staff are responsive and helpful
- Current students look engaged

**Bottom line:** PTC is NOT a scam, but it's also not a substitute for doing your homework. Get the data, talk to people, and decide based on facts—not just marketing messaging.
