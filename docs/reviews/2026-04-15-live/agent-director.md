## Top 3 Issues (Ranked by Institutional Risk)

### 1. CRITICAL: Accreditation Documentation Incomplete (Risk: COE Compliance & Board Reporting)

**Issue:** Footer accreditation text is truncated and lacks full institutional context. Accreditation pages exist but are not easily discoverable from main navigation. For board members and COE visitors, the message "Individually accredited by COE" appears incomplete without direct links to:
- Current accreditation status/documents
- Last COE visit report
- Next scheduled review date
- Standards compliance evidence

**Impact:** Board members reviewing the site may question thoroughness. COE site visitors may struggle to find self-study reports or institutional planning documents. PCSB leadership cannot quickly verify accreditation standing.

**Recommendation:**
- Create homepage footer banner with full accreditation statement and link to dedicated "Accreditation" page
- Ensure "COE" is clickable and links to current COE accreditation profile
- Add brief compliance/standards statement to "About Us" sections on both campuses
- Consider adding "Accreditation Documents" library in admissions section

---

### 2. HIGH: Campus Identity & Market Differentiation Weak (Risk: Enrollment & Retention)

**Issue:** Both campuses have similar navigation, layout, and homepage messaging. Without clear value propositions, prospective students cannot distinguish between Clearwater and St. Petersburg offerings. St. Petersburg's strong apprenticeship programs and Clearwater's broader technical programs are not marketed distinctly.

**Current state:** "Opportunity Starts Here" works for both. But:
- Why choose Clearwater over St. Petersburg for a nursing program?
- Why choose St. Petersburg for a welding apprenticeship?
- Are there geographic advantages? Employer partnerships? Unique program sequences?

**Impact:** Reduces conversion rates. Increases enrollment friction. May dilute PCSB's ability to distribute students effectively across campuses. Enrollment announcements (National Signing Day) treat campuses as interchangeable.

**Recommendation:**
- Develop campus-specific personas and value propositions
- Clearwater: "Broader program portfolio, hands-on labs, central location"
- St. Petersburg: "Specialized trades and apprenticeships, strong employer partnerships, community focus"
- Refresh hero imagery to show campus-specific environments (buildings, equipment, students)
- Add "Why [Campus Name]?" section to each homepage
- Create program comparison tool if both campuses offer same program (e.g., Welding)

---

### 3. HIGH: Compliance & Legal Statements Scattered & Incomplete (Risk: Legal Liability & Accessibility)

**Issue:** Non-discrimination, Title IX, Section 504, ADA, and sexual misconduct disclosures are spread across multiple pages and footer links. The primary compliance page on the main site is plain-text, poorly formatted, and contains only CTAE/EOO information. Missing or unclear statements on:
- Title IX coordinator and reporting process
- Section 504 complaint procedures
- ADA accommodations process
- Sexual harassment grievance procedures
- Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) summary

**Current state:** Sexual misconduct/predator notice exists but compliance officer contact is on separate page. Accessibility statement is in footer utility (hard to find). Different campus sites have inconsistent structures.

**Impact:** Legal exposure if a student cannot easily find how to report discrimination. Accessibility audit may flag missing WCAG compliance statements. Board liability if notices are not prominently displayed.

**Recommendation:**
- Create consolidated "Legal & Compliance" page on main site with clear sections:
  - Non-Discrimination Statement (prominently displayed)
  - Title IX / Sexual Misconduct Reporting
  - Section 504 / ADA Accommodations
  - FERPA / Student Records
  - Compliance Officer Contact
- Link this page from main navigation under "About Us" or "Legal"
- Ensure same structure on both campus sites
- Update accessibility statement with WCAG 2.1 AA commitment
- Add campus-specific advisor contact for accommodations (not generic email)
- Consider adding "Report a Concern" button to main footer for quick access

---

## Summary Assessment

PTC's website demonstrates solid foundational structure with clear navigation, comprehensive program listings, and good enrollment CTAs. Finalsite platform is stable and responsive. However, institutional presentation lags behind competitor vocational schools in three critical areas:

1. **Institutional gravitas:** Accreditation and compliance messaging needs prominence and clarity for board/visitor confidence
2. **Market positioning:** Campus differentiation is weak; enrollment conversion would improve with clearer value propositions
3. **Compliance/Legal:** Scattered notices create liability risk; consolidation needed for accessibility and legal compliance

Recommended focus: **Q2 2026 accreditation refresh** + **Q3 2026 campus differentiation redesign** before July transition to Distance Learning Coordinator role.
