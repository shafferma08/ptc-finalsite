About This Program

A 750-hour planned sequence of advanced welding courses for students who have completed Welding Technology or who bring equivalent industry experience.

The Welding Technology - Advanced program prepares students for higher-skill welding roles in pipe fabrication, structural construction, and advanced manufacturing. Coursework focuses on pipe welding processes, blueprint reading and layout, weld inspection and quality, and the fabrication techniques used in shipyard, refinery, and heavy-construction settings.

Graduates are prepared to sit for the American Welding Society (AWS) certification tests required by employers in pipe and pressure-vessel work, and continue to earn the Florida Ready to Work credentials that complement PTC's career and technical programs.

Course Sequence

A 750-hour planned sequence focused on pipe welding, layout, and advanced fabrication. Courses are taken in order.

Pipe Welder 1 ~150 Hours

Introduction to pipe welding processes for students who have already mastered structural welding. Coverage includes pipe joint preparation, fit-up, root pass technique, and an introduction to position welding (2G, 5G).

Pipe Welder 2 ~150 Hours

Builds on Pipe Welder 1 with intermediate position welding (6G), open-root work, and multi-pass technique on schedule 40 and schedule 80 carbon-steel pipe.

Layout & Fabrication ~150 Hours

Blueprint reading, weld symbols, fit-up techniques, and the planning and execution of fabrication projects. Students work from drawings to finished assemblies.

Advanced Welder ~300 Hours

Capstone studio focused on advanced GTAW (TIG), GMAW (MIG), and FCAW techniques on pipe and plate. Includes weld inspection, quality assurance, and preparation for the AWS certification test in the student's chosen process and position.

Course titles and hour distribution may be adjusted by campus. Confirm the current sequence with your counselor.

Industry Credentials

American Welding Society (AWS)

Graduates are prepared to test for AWS certification in their chosen process and position. Both the Clearwater and St. Petersburg campuses are AWS accredited testing facilities, so students can complete their performance qualification tests on the same site where they trained. AWS application fees apply.

Florida Ready to Work

PTC's career and technical programs expect students to earn the FL Ready to Work Soft Skills credential before graduating. The Digital Skills and Employability Skills credentials are also encouraged.

These credentials align with our BIG SIX initiative: professionalism, pride, focus, success, ownership, and excellence. myfloridareadytowork.com →

Financial Aid & Costs

Florida Resident tuition is $2.91/clock hour for advanced welding (subject to change). Lab fees and consumables are billed separately. Financial aid is available to those who qualify.

Career Outlook

Where Advanced Welders Work

Pipe welders, structural welders, and welder-fabricators are in steady demand across Florida's shipbuilding, refinery, utility, and heavy-construction industries.

$47,540+

Median pay for welders

U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2023. Pipe and structural welders typically earn above the median.

Projected job growth

Annual openings (U.S.)

Common job titles for graduates include Pipe Welder, Structural Welder, Welder-Fabricator, Combo Welder, and Welding Inspector (entry level). Many graduates pursue work in shipyards, power generation, and pipeline construction.
