Consumer Information & Disclosures

Federal, state, and accreditation requirements call on colleges to publish certain information to help students and families make informed decisions. This page brings all of our required disclosures together in one place.

Pinellas Technical College (PTC) is a career and technical college operated by the Pinellas County School Board (PCSB). As a postsecondary institution receiving federal student aid and accredited by the Council on Occupational Education (COE), PTC is required to make the following consumer information publicly available. If you would like a paper copy of any document linked on this page, please contact the campus of your choice and one will be provided free of charge.

Accreditation

Pinellas Technical College is accredited by multiple external bodies. Students and prospective students are entitled to information about PTC's accreditation status, and may review accreditation documents on request.

Non-Discrimination & Title IX

Pinellas County Public Schools · Career Technical & Adult Education

Career Technical & Adult Education School and Programs are open to all eligible students in the district and is committed to a policy of nondiscrimination in employment and educational opportunity. No persons shall be discriminated against or harassed in any educational program, services or activities on the basis of race, color, sex, religion, national origin, marital status, age, sexual orientation or disability.

Admission is open to students with limited English proficiency. In order to eliminate barriers, the district assesses each student's ability to participate and benefit through placement testing and counseling. Based on assessment, services or referrals are provided to help prepare students for successful participation.

Sexual Misconduct & Sexual Predators

Sexual Misconduct

Pinellas Technical College is committed to providing an environment which is free from all forms of sexual misconduct including sexual harassment, public indecency, voyeurism, battery, or assault. Anyone accused of such charges may be subject to dismissal from the college. Victims must contact the director of their campus (Clearwater - 727.538.7167 or St. Petersburg - 727.893.2500), and they will be provided guidance, assistance and support.

Sexual Predators

Information regarding sexual predators or offenders attending or employed by an institution of higher learning may be obtained from the local law enforcement agency or the Department of Corrections. The Florida Department of Law Enforcement has established a hotline (1-888-FL-PREDATOR) or (1-888-357-7332) that allows the public to request information about sexual predators and sex offenders living in their communities and around the state. Requests may be made between the hours of 8 a.m. and 7 p.m. You may also visit the FDLE website for sexual predator photographs and the 2002 Campus Sex Crimes Prevention Act.
