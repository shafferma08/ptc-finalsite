Consumer Information & Disclosures

Federal, state, and accreditation requirements call on colleges to publish certain information to help students and families make informed decisions. This page brings all of our required disclosures together in one place.

Pinellas Technical College (PTC) is a career and technical college operated by the Pinellas County School Board (PCSB). As a postsecondary institution receiving federal student aid and accredited by the Council on Occupational Education (COE), PTC is required to make the following consumer information publicly available. If you would like a paper copy of any document linked on this page, please contact the campus of your choice and one will be provided free of charge.

Accreditation

Pinellas Technical College is accredited by multiple external bodies. Students and prospective students are entitled to information about PTC's accreditation status, and may review accreditation documents on request.

Council on Occupational Education (COE)

PTC's career and technical programs are accredited by COE, a national accrediting agency recognized by the U.S. Department of Education.

Visit council.org

Cognia

As part of Pinellas County Schools, PTC is accredited by Cognia (formerly AdvancED / SACS CASI), covering K–12 and adult education.

Visit cognia.org

To request a copy of the COE accreditation report or institutional self-study, please contact the compliance office listed at the bottom of this page.

Non-Discrimination & Title IX

Pinellas County Schools prohibits any and all forms of discrimination and harassment based on race, color, sex, religion, national origin, marital status, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, pregnancy, or disability in its educational programs, services, activities, or employment.

Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972 protects individuals from discrimination based on sex in any education program or activity receiving federal financial assistance. This includes sexual harassment and sexual violence.

Reporting a concern: Any student, employee, or visitor who believes they have experienced or witnessed discrimination or harassment is encouraged to report it promptly. Reports may be made to a campus administrator or directly to the district's Office of Equity.

Privacy & FERPA

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. Once a student turns 18 or enrolls in a postsecondary institution, FERPA rights transfer from the parent to the student.

Under FERPA, eligible students have the right to:

  • Inspect and review their own education records
  • Request that inaccurate or misleading records be corrected
  • Consent before personally identifiable information is disclosed, except where FERPA allows disclosure without consent
  • File a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education concerning alleged failures by PTC to comply with FERPA

Directory information: PTC may disclose "directory information" without written consent unless a student has opted out. Directory information may include name, dates of attendance, credential earned, and participation in officially recognized activities. Students may request in writing that their directory information be withheld.

For the full PCSB privacy policy, visit pcsb.org.

Accessibility

PTC is committed to ensuring that its website, facilities, programs, and services are accessible to individuals with disabilities in accordance with Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and applicable state law.

Website accessibility: This website is being designed and developed to meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. We regularly audit pages and components for contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen reader compatibility.

Requesting an accommodation: Students who need academic accommodations, accessible materials, or physical accommodations should contact their campus Student Services office as early as possible, ideally before the start of a program. Accommodations are determined on an individual basis.

Reporting an accessibility barrier: If you encounter a page, document, or physical space that is not accessible to you, please let us know. We will work with you to provide the information or service in an alternate format.

Student Outcomes & Right to Know

COE-accredited institutions are required to publish data on program completion, job placement, and, where applicable, licensure pass rates. PTC publishes these figures annually and reports them to our accrediting agency.

Outcome data available on request includes:

  • Program completion rates
  • Job placement rates in the field of study
  • Licensure pass rates for programs leading to state-issued credentials (e.g., nursing, cosmetology, barbering)
  • Retention rates
Data refresh: The most recent COE Annual Report outcome data is posted when published by the agency. Contact the Distance Learning Coordinator or campus Student Services office to request the current report.

Financial Aid Disclosures

Detailed financial aid information is published on our Tuition & Financial Aid page. Required federal disclosures for Title IV participants include:

  • Cost of Attendance: Tuition rates are published at tuition-aid.html#rates. Florida Resident: $2.91/clock hour. Non-Resident: $11.64/clock hour.
  • Net Price Calculator: Campus-specific calculators are available on the Tuition & Aid page.
  • Refund Policy: Published in the PTC student handbook and summarized on the Tuition & Aid FAQ.
  • Federal School Code: 013847 (for FAFSA filing).
  • Withdrawal and Return of Title IV Funds: Students who withdraw before completing more than 60% of the payment period may be required to return a portion of federal aid received. Full policy available on request.
  • Verification: Students selected for verification by the U.S. Department of Education will be notified and asked to provide supporting documentation before aid is finalized.

Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

Students receiving federal or state financial aid must meet Satisfactory Academic Progress standards to maintain eligibility. SAP is evaluated on both qualitative (grades) and quantitative (pace of completion) measures.

Key standards include:

  • Minimum grade requirement per program
  • Minimum pace of completion (measured as clock hours completed vs. attempted)
  • Maximum timeframe to complete the program, not to exceed 150% of the published program length

Students who fall below SAP standards may be placed on warning or suspended from aid, and may appeal with documented extenuating circumstances. Full SAP policy is available in the PTC student handbook and from campus financial aid offices.

Campus Security & Clery Act

The Jeanne Clery Act requires postsecondary institutions participating in federal financial aid programs to disclose information about crime on and near campus and to publish an Annual Security Report.

PTC's Annual Security Report includes:

  • Crime statistics for the previous three calendar years for specified categories of offenses
  • Campus policies related to emergency response, timely warnings, and sexual assault prevention
  • Information about law enforcement authority and relationships with local agencies
  • Drug and alcohol policies and prevention programs

Printed copies of the Annual Security Report are available free of charge at the front desk of either campus. An electronic copy may be requested by emailing campus administration.

Drug & Alcohol Abuse Prevention

Under the Drug-Free Schools and Communities Act, PTC prohibits the unlawful possession, use, or distribution of illicit drugs and alcohol by students and employees on PTC premises or as part of any PTC activity.

PTC's drug and alcohol policy addresses:

  • Standards of conduct for students and employees
  • Legal sanctions under federal, state, and local law
  • Health risks associated with substance abuse
  • Counseling, treatment, and rehabilitation resources available
  • Disciplinary sanctions for violations, up to and including dismissal and referral to law enforcement

The full policy is published in the PTC student handbook and reviewed annually. Free prevention resources are available through PCSB Student Services.

Copyright & Peer-to-Peer File Sharing

PTC and Pinellas County Schools comply with U.S. copyright law (Title 17, U.S. Code) and the Higher Education Opportunity Act's provisions regarding peer-to-peer (P2P) file sharing.

Unauthorized distribution of copyrighted material, including peer-to-peer file sharing of music, movies, software, or games, may subject students to civil and criminal liabilities. Penalties can include actual damages plus profits, or statutory damages between $750 and $30,000 per work infringed (up to $150,000 for willful infringement), plus attorneys' fees and potential criminal charges.

PTC employs technical measures on campus networks to deter unauthorized P2P distribution and provides students with information about legal alternatives for obtaining music, video, and software.

Voter Registration

Under the Higher Education Act, institutions of higher education must make a good-faith effort to distribute voter registration forms to each enrolled student.

Florida voter registration forms are available at both campuses at Student Services. Students may also register online at registertovoteflorida.gov.

Constitution Day

Each year on or around September 17, PTC observes Constitution Day with programming that commemorates the signing of the U.S. Constitution in 1787. Educational resources are made available to students and staff during the observance.
